About

Rammstein is a German industrial metal band formed in 1989 by Richard Z. Kruspe (Lead guitar, backup singer), Other band members include Till Lindemann (Lead singer), Christian Lorenz (Keyboard), Christoph Schneider (Drums, percussion), Paul Landers (Rythm guitar, backup vocals) , and Oliver Riedel (Bass guitar, backup vocals).

Notability

Rammstein is known for using pyrotechnics on stage. In their last performance, lead singer Till Lindemann, attached two flamethrowers to his forearms and shot flames over the crowd. One of their most famous uses of flames was when Lindemann attached ten foot wings to his back and shot flames out of them as well. They're liked so much because they like to "push" the boundaries and not care what people think.

History

Each band member was part of a small band before they banded (no pun intended) together and formed "Rammstein". They're named after a Rammsteine, which is a doorstop device found on old gates. The extra "m" in the band's name makes it translate literally as "ramming stone."

Popularity

Rammstein got their first record deal after they won a "battle of the bands" type of ordeal. After this, they toured with a few bands and such as Clawfinger and Prague. Over the years they've obtained more and more followers and completely sells out every show.
